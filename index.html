Atal Bihari Vajpayee

1924 - 2018

Atal Bihari Vajpayee, a towering figure in Indian politics, served as the 10th Prime Minister of India. Born on December 25, 1924, in Gwalior, he dedicated over six decades of his life to public service. Vajpayee was a charismatic leader, renowned for his oratorical skills and statesmanship.

A founding member of the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), Vajpayee played a pivotal role in shaping the party's ideology. He rose to prominence as a parliamentarian and became the first non-Congress Prime Minister to complete a full term in office from 1998 to 2004. His tenure saw significant economic reforms, nuclear tests in Pokhran, and improved relations with the United States.

Vajpayee's leadership extended beyond political boundaries, earning him respect globally. His efforts to foster peace in the Indian subcontinent, notably the historic bus journey to Lahore in 1999, aimed at improving relations with Pakistan. The 'Golden Quadrilateral' project, connecting major cities through a network of highways, showcased his commitment to infrastructure development.

Biographics

  • Born on December 25, 1924, in Gwalior, Madhya Pradesh, India.
  • Belonged to a middle-class Brahmin family.
  • Studied at Victoria College, Gwalior, and later graduated from DAV College, Kanpur.
  • Active participant in the Quit India Movement in 1942.
  • Joined the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS) and later became a member of the Bharatiya Jana Sangh (BJS).
  • Elected to the Lok Sabha for the first time in 1957.
